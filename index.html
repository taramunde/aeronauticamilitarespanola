Key Milestones

1911
Royal decree establishes the Servicio de Aeronautica Militar within the Army Corps of Engineers. First Farman MF.7 biplanes arrive at Cuatro Vientos airfield near Madrid. Captain Alfredo Kindelan makes the first official military flight.
1912
Formation of the 1st Escuadrilla under Captain Eduardo Barron. Nieuport IV monoplanes join the fleet. The Escuela de Vuelo (Flight School) is formally organized at Cuatro Vientos.
1913
Official creation of the Servicio de Aeronautica Militar by royal decree (April). First combat missions flown in the Rif War, Morocco, using Farman MF.11 aircraft. The escuadrilla expedicionaria operates from Tetuan.
1914
Continued operations in Morocco. Acquisition of Morane-Saulnier and Caudron types. Spain remains neutral in World War I but closely observes European air developments.
1915
First bombing raids against Rif positions. Farman F.40 pushers enter service. Reconnaissance missions intensify over the Moroccan front. Captain Barron conducts pioneering long-range flights.
1916-17
Major fleet expansion with Caudron G.4 twin-engine bombers and Avro 504 trainers. May 1917: creation of the Aeronautica Naval under the Spanish Navy. FBA flying boats become the first naval aircraft.
1918
SPAD VII and Hanriot HD.1 fighters enter service, bringing modern fighter capability. Breguet XIV bombers acquired for the Army squadrons. Intense combat operations continue in Morocco.
1919
Post-war reorganization and modernization. De Havilland DH.4 bombers and naval flying boats expand capabilities. Preparations begin for the first Spanish transatlantic flight attempt. Infrastructure expands with new airfields.

The Rif War 1913-1919

The Spanish Protectorate in Morocco became the crucible where Spanish military aviation was forged. From 1913 onward, aircraft conducted reconnaissance, artillery spotting, bombing, and close air support missions against Rif tribal forces. The harsh North African environment tested both men and machines to their limits.

Northern Morocco, Spanish Protectorate
Tetuan, Larache, Alhucemas

Operations Timeline

  • 1913First combat sorties from Tetuan airfield. Farman MF.11 aircraft conduct reconnaissance over rebel positions in the western Rif.
  • 1914Expanded operations including aerial photography for cartography. First instances of dropping grenades and flechettes from aircraft.
  • 1915Systematic bombing raids begin against Rif encampments. Farman F.40 aircraft provide improved bomb-carrying capability.
  • 1916Caudron G.4 twin-engine bombers deliver heavier payloads. Aircraft losses mount due to ground fire and mechanical failures in harsh conditions.
  • 1917Naval aviation joins operations with FBA flying boats conducting coastal patrols from Larache and Ceuta.
  • 1918Modern fighters arrive: SPAD VII and Hanriot HD.1 provide air superiority. Breguet XIV bombers significantly increase strike capability.
  • 1919Post-war consolidation. Operations continue with modernized fleet. Spanish aviation gains invaluable combat experience for future conflicts.
